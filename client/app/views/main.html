The Deep Space Climate Observatory (DSCOVR) is the replacement for the NASA Advanced Composition Explorer (ACE) to monitor space weather at L1. DSCOVR data enables forecasters to observe space weather conditions and supports research investigations. The results of these efforts will be warning and understanding of conditions that could disrupt Earth-based infrastructure.

Here, data are archived, distributed, and visualized for magnetometer and faraday cup measurements. For realtime data, see the Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC). For EPIC and NISTAR data, see NASA's Atmospheric Science Data Center.

Space Weather Instruments

DSCOVR is carrying the following space weather monitoring instruments:

Nickname Name Purpose
PlasMag Solar Wind Plasma Sensor and Magnetometer Measure solar wind velocity, magnitude and direction of the solar wind magnetic filed
PHA Pulse Height Analyzer Provide real-time measurement of particle events that may impact DSCOVR's electronics

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is DSCOVR?
DSCOVR is the Deep Space Climate Observatory. See DSCOVR Home.

Where can I access realtime solar wind data from DSCOVR?
See NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC).
Where can I access EPIC or NISTAR data?
See NASA's Atmospheric Science Data Center.
Why do the time series plots slow down my computer?
The time series plots are resource intensive. A high volume of data and interactivity contribute to this. We recommend reducing the number of panes, lines, or span of time you have plotted. We have not imposed any limits on the number of panes you may create in the time series under the philosophy that no limit is suitable for everyone's hardware capabilities.
How do I share my custom plot?
If you created a plot, it can be uniquely identified and shared by the URL.
How do I embed a plot I have created into a presentation?
Take a screen shot of the plot if you wish to embed it in a presentation.
How do I obtain publication quality plots?
See Visualize Data. This site permits you to download the data associated with any custom visualization you create, so that you can generate a relevant plot with your own software if you prefer.
How do I find interesting events to visualize?
See "find events" module under Visualize Data, and search the entire dataset.
