The Deep Space Climate Observatory (DSCOVR) is the replacement satellite for the NASA Advanced Composition Explorer (ACE) spacecraft to continue monitoring space weather from L1. DSCOVR allows forecasters to observe space weather conditions and supports research investigations. The results of these efforts will be warning and understanding of conditions that could disrupt Earth-based infrastructure.

Here, data are archived, distributed, and visualized for magnetometer and faraday cup measurements. For realtime data, see the Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC). For EPIC and NISTAR data, see NASA's Atmospheric Science Data Center.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is DSCOVR?
DSCOVR is the Deep Space Climate Observatory. See DSCOVR Home for general information.
What is the earliest DSCOVR space weather data I can access?
Space weather data will become releasable at a date to be determined by National Weather Service. Historical DSCOVR data may be reprocessed back to a date where the hardware reached its final configuration at the discretion of the data provider.
Where can I access realtime solar wind data from DSCOVR?
See NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC).
Where can I access EPIC or NISTAR data?
See NASA's Atmospheric Science Data Center.
Where can I download full resolution data, raw telemetry, or to make bulk orders?
Please visit our Next Data order interface.
How can I create my own machine to machine interface to acquire archived data?
The NCEI Agile Team created an interface that permits access to all publicly available DSCOVR data. If you are comfortable interacting with a Rest API, the relevant documentation is available at the following link: Next Data order interface.
How do I share my custom plot?
The URL displayed for your plot can be shared with others. Additionally, a .png format image can be downloaded for each plot by using an option in the upper right corner of the plot.
How do I embed a plot I have created into a presentation?
We recommend you download a .png image of your plot by clicking the option in the upper right hand corner of the plot.
How do I obtain publication quality plots?
See Visualize Data. This site permits you to download the data associated with any custom visualization you create, so that you can generate a relevant plot with your own software if you prefer.
How can I improve the performance of the visualization pages?
If you are experiencing performance issues, please reduce the volume of data plotted by either shortening the time span or reducing the number of variables requested. We have not limited the length of plots or number of panels due to varying end user hardware capabilities.
What is the difference between synchronous (portal) vs. tape archive (next) access?
Files served via this portal are stored on hard drives in our data center and you may browse and retrieve them without delay. Files available only through the tape archive have been stored on magnetic tapes. To download these, you must make a request, and a reader will read the tapes to collect the files you requested. The next service will send you an email when the files are ready to download.
